# Gies AI Integration Strategy

**Status:** Active Implementation (Fall 2026 Launch) | **Updated:** January 4, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Gies is implementing comprehensive AI integration in undergraduate curriculum by Fall 2026. **Goal:** 100% of graduates develop AI literacy and practical competencies.

**Key Initiatives:**
- **3 new courses:** AI Governance (315), AI Tools (212), IT/AI Strategy (350, enhanced from BADM 449)
- **6 existing courses enhanced** with AI modules
- **L-C-E progression:** Literacy → Competency → Expertise
- **Aligned with campus AI framework:** 4 tracks

**Timeline:** Spring 2026 design | Fall 2026 launch | **Investment:** Low (building on existing)

## Learning Outcomes Framework (L-C-E)

### Literacy Level
Students understand:
- What AI is and how it works
- Where AI creates business value
- AI limitations, risks, and ethical considerations
- AI's impact on their industry

### Competency Level
Students can:
- Use AI tools effectively (ChatGPT, Gemini, APIs)
- Apply AI to solve real business problems
- Evaluate organizational readiness for AI adoption
- Design human-AI workflows
- Navigate governance and responsible AI practices

### Expertise Level (Future 400-level)
Students can:
- Develop AI transformation strategies
- Lead cross-functional AI initiatives
- Build custom AI solutions (low-code/no-code)
- Design AI governance frameworks

## Strategic Alignment

### Campus AI Framework (4 Tracks)
1. **AI Basics/Fundamentals** — Gies provides business domain electives
2. **AI & ML Technologies** — Business-specific ML applications
3. **Agentic Systems & Workflows** — Business process automation (Gies leadership)
4. **Human Centric AI** — Business ethics, governance, policy

### Gies Contribution Model
- Offer courses as electives in campus AI Fundamentals
- Develop Agentic AI for Business leadership track
- Position Gies as business domain expert
