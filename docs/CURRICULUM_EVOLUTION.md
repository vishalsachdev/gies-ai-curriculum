# Curriculum Evolution: New Courses & Course Redesigns

**Date:** January 4, 2026 | **Scope:** BADM Core Curriculum Enhancement Strategy | **Framework:** Literacy-Competency-Expertise (L-C-E) Progression

---

## Executive Summary

The current BADM core curriculum (7 courses) provides strong foundational AI coverage but has strategic gaps:

- **Only 1 Expertise-level course** (BADM 449) vs. need for 3-4 advanced offerings
- **Human Centric AI underweighted** (2 courses vs. importance of governance/ethics/workforce impact)
- **No dedicated AI governance course** despite regulatory acceleration (EU AI Act, US policy frameworks)
- **No custom AI development pathway** (prompt engineering, API integration, low-code platforms)
- **Limited vertical specialization** (AI in Finance, AI in Operations, etc.)

**Recommendation:** Add 5-7 new courses and rename 4-5 existing courses to create a comprehensive AI leadership program.

## üÜï NEW COURSES TO CREATE

### **Tier 1: IMMEDIATE (Fall 2026 Pilot)**

#### **1. BADM 315: AI Governance & Responsible Business**
- **Level:** 300 (Junior/Senior)
- **Track:** Human Centric AI
- **L-C-E Target:** Literacy ‚Üí Competency
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None (or BADM 210 recommended)

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Fills critical gap in AI governance and responsible AI practices
- Differentiates Gies from peer institutions (most focus on AI/ML, few on governance)
- Aligns with regulatory acceleration (EU AI Act, US executive orders, GDPR)
- Supports "Zero AI Barriers" strategic goal (everyone understands risks)
- Prepares students for increasingly liability-conscious boardrooms

**Course Content:**
- **Week 1-3:** AI ethics frameworks (fairness, transparency, accountability), bias detection and mitigation
- **Week 4-6:** Regulatory landscape (EU AI Act, US policy frameworks, GDPR, sector-specific rules)
- **Week 7-9:** Organizational AI governance structures, oversight mechanisms, accountability models
- **Week 10-12:** Case studies (Meta content moderation, Zillow's ML failure, Amazon hiring bias) + capstone governance framework project

**Learning Outcomes (L-C-E):**
- **Literacy:** Identify AI governance concerns; explain regulatory requirements; recognize organizational oversight structures
- **Competency:** Apply ethical frameworks to AI decisions; evaluate governance adequacy; design accountability mechanisms

**Who Should Teach:** BADM 300 instructor (legal) + Guest lecturer from tech compliance/governance

**Implementation Timeline:** Spring 2026 course design ‚Üí Fall 2026 pilot

---

#### **2. BADM 212: AI Tools for Business Problem Solving**
- **Level:** 200 (Sophomore)
- **Track:** AI Basics ‚Üí Agentic Systems
- **L-C-E Target:** Competency
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** BADM 210 or equivalent

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Bridges between AI literacy (BADM 210) and specialized applications (BADM 275, 320, 449)
- Teaches applied skills students explicitly demand (prompt engineering, API use, automation)
- Creates explicit "agentic systems" pathway (211 ‚Üí 212 ‚Üí 275/320/449)
- Differentiator: Most business schools teach theoretical AI; Gies teaches application
- Hands-on course improves engagement and retention

**Course Content:**
- **Week 1-3:** Prompt engineering best practices, model selection, evaluation metrics
- **Week 4-6:** LLM APIs in practice (OpenAI, Google, Anthropic), authentication, rate limiting, cost optimization
- **Week 7-9:** Building agentic workflows (multi-step processes, tool use, error handling, human-in-the-loop)
- **Week 10-12:** Student-built mini-applications
  - Text summarization + Q&A system
  - Data extraction from unstructured documents
  - Conversational research assistant
  - Email classification + response drafting

**Learning Outcomes (C level):**
- **Competency:** Evaluate and select appropriate AI tools; engineer effective prompts; integrate APIs into workflows; design human-AI collaboration patterns

**Who Should Teach:** BADM 210/211 instructor (analytics) with TA support

**Implementation Timeline:** Spring 2026 design ‚Üí Fall 2026 launch

---

#### **3. BADM 410: AI Strategy & Competitive Advantage** (Rename BADM 449)
- **Current:** BADM 449 - Business Policy & Strategy
- **Proposed:** Rebrand as "AI Strategy & Organizational Transformation"
- **Level:** 400 (Senior capstone)
- **Track:** Agentic Systems
- **L-C-E Target:** Competency ‚Üí Expertise
- **Credits:** 3

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Course already covers strategy; making AI focus explicit positions Gies as AI-first
- Signals to employers and students that strategy = AI strategy
- Differentiates from generic "business policy" courses at other schools
- Capstone course is perfect venue for comprehensive AI transformation strategy project
- Aligns with Haas (Berkeley) positioning

**New Course Focus:**
- **Week 1-3:** How AI creates/disrupts competitive advantage; technology adoption S-curves; case studies (Tesla, Google, Amazon, Stripe)
- **Week 4-6:** Organizational readiness for AI (talent needs, infrastructure, data maturity); failure modes of AI adoption
- **Week 7-9:** AI-enabled business model innovation; examples across industries
- **Week 10-12:** Capstone: Students design AI transformation strategy for assigned company
  - Board-ready executive summary
  - Technical feasibility assessment
  - Organizational change management
  - Risk/governance framework

**Learning Outcomes (C‚ÜíE):**
- **Competency:** Analyze competitive positioning using AI; evaluate AI adoption readiness; formulate strategic choices
- **Expertise:** Create transformation strategies; lead cross-functional AI initiatives; innovate business models

**Who Should Teach:** Current 449 instructor with reinforced AI focus

**Implementation Timeline:** Summer 2026 course redesign ‚Üí Fall 2026 launch as BADM 410

### **Tier 2: SPRING 2027**

#### **4. BADM 311: AI & Workforce Transformation**
- **Level:** 300 (Junior/Senior)
- **Track:** Human Centric AI
- **L-C-E Target:** Literacy ‚Üí Competency
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** None (complements BADM 310)

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Enhances BADM 310 (Management & Organizational Behavior) with AI workforce impact
- Critical for MBA/executive programs; talent/HR teams drive AI adoption
- Addresses McKinsey research: "Fear of job displacement" is #1 barrier to AI adoption
- Supports "1,000 AI Leaders" goal (workforce transformation leaders)
- Emerging regulatory focus (EU requirements on workforce impact assessment)

**Course Content:**
- **Week 1-3:** Job displacement projections by industry; reskilling case studies; transition pathways
- **Week 4-6:** Human-AI collaboration in team design; workflow redesign; productivity gains from human-AI pairs
- **Week 7-9:** Change management for AI adoption; leadership behaviors; psychological safety; trust in AI systems
- **Week 10-12:** Equity and inclusion in AI transformation; disproportionate impact on underrepresented groups; mitigation strategies

**Learning Outcomes:**
- **Literacy:** Recognize AI impact on job roles; explain reskilling pathways; identify equity concerns
- **Competency:** Design human-AI workflows; lead change management; assess workforce impact; develop transition plans

**Who Should Teach:** BADM 310 instructor with I/O Psychology background

**Implementation Timeline:** Fall 2026 design ‚Üí Spring 2027 launch

### **Tier 3: FUTURE (2028+) - Advanced Electives & Specializations**

#### **5. BADM 420: Practical AI Development**
- **Level:** 400 (Senior elective)
- **Track:** Agentic Systems
- **L-C-E Target:** Expertise
- **Credits:** 3
- **Prerequisites:** BADM 212, BADM 211

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Teaches low-code/no-code platforms (Make.com, Zapier, Azure Logic Apps) for non-engineers
- Builds on BADM 212 foundation
- Gives students production-ready portfolio piece
- Differentiator: Practical tooling, not just theory

**Course Focus:**
- Building multi-step automated workflows
- Connecting AI services (OpenAI, Google Vision, etc.)
- Error handling and monitoring
- Cost optimization and scaling
- Student projects: Invoice automation, customer service bot, lead scoring system

**Implementation Timeline:** 2027 design ‚Üí Fall 2028 launch

#### **6. BADM 421-series: AI in [Domain] Specializations**
- **Level:** 400 (Senior electives)
- **Track:** Agentic Systems + Domain-specific
- **L-C-E Target:** Expertise
- **Credits:** 3 each
- **Examples:**
  - **BADM 421F: AI in Finance** (Algorithmic trading, fraud detection, credit risk, portfolio optimization)
  - **BADM 421M: AI in Marketing** (Customer lifetime value prediction, churn prediction, personalization at scale)
  - **BADM 421O: AI in Operations** (Demand forecasting, supplier optimization, quality control automation)
  - **BADM 421H: AI in HR** (Talent acquisition, employee retention prediction, compensation optimization)

**Strategic Rationale:**
- Addresses "AI in domain areas" gap
- Vertical expertise commands premium in job market
- Aligns with student interests (finance, marketing, supply chain)
- Draws from domain faculty (Finance prof teaches 421F, etc.)

**Implementation Timeline:** 2027-2028 course design ‚Üí Rolling launch 2028+
